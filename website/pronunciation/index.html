What does Chau7 mean?

The name Chau7 is a wordplay on the French word chaussette, meaning "sock".

chau + 7 (sept) = chaussette = 🧦

Sept is the French word for 7. Stick it onto chau and you get chaussette, the common French word for sock. Computer science has never been this cozy.

Why is Chau7 named after a sock?

Chau7 is named after chaussette, a running gag from Le Donjon de Naheulbeuk, a cult French MP3 audio saga from the 2000s. Some apps are named after Greek gods. Others after celestial phenomena. Chau7 went with an insider joke from a fantasy audio comedy.

If you grew up in France in the 2000s, you know Le Donjon de Naheulbeuk: a hilariously chaotic fantasy audio saga that an entire generation downloaded on eMule and passed around on burned CDs. It's the French equivalent of discovering Monty Python, except everyone has a D20 and nobody agrees on initiative order.

In the saga, chaussette is absurd, out of place, and unforgettable. If you know, you know. If you don't, you just downloaded a terminal named after a sock, and that's also pretty funny.

The name Chau7 is also a seven-letter puzzle hiding the number 7. If you're going to name a developer tool, you might as well make it a riddle. And this time it's not a bar… but that would be a great bar name.
