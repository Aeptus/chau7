High Contrast Mode

Respects macOS High Contrast. Sharper borders, stronger text, automatic adaptation.

The problem

  • Low-contrast terminal chrome and overlays are hard to use in bright or visually demanding environments.
  • Accessibility is not solved by a theme if critical states are still visually subtle.

What Chau7 does about it

  • Emphasizes stronger text, borders, and state contrast for readability.
  • Makes overlays, badges, and controls easier to distinguish at a glance.
  • Lives within the native appearance system rather than a separate web-style skin.
  • Supports accessibility-minded setups alongside fonts, themes, and reduced motion.

What is High Contrast Mode in Chau7?

High Contrast Mode in Chau7 automatically detects the macOS High Contrast accessibility setting and enhances the visual presentation of the entire terminal. Chau7 increases border prominence, text contrast, and UI element visibility.

Chau7 applies high contrast enhancements to both the terminal content area and all chrome elements. Selection highlighting, cursor visibility, focus rings, and interactive controls all receive enhanced contrast treatment in Chau7.

Do I need to configure high contrast separately in Chau7?

No. Chau7 automatically detects the macOS High Contrast accessibility setting and adapts its rendering. No per-app configuration is needed.

When you enable High Contrast in macOS System Settings, Chau7 picks up the change immediately. Chau7 enhances borders around windows, tabs, split panes, and UI controls without any manual setup.

Does high contrast mode in Chau7 affect my theme?

Chau7 adjusts theme colors to improve contrast while preserving the overall character of your chosen color scheme. The goal is readability enhancement, not a complete visual override.

Text rendering in Chau7 shifts to prioritize contrast over aesthetics, ensuring every character is maximally distinguishable from its background. Your theme remains recognizable while becoming more readable.

How does Chau7's high contrast mode compare to other terminals?

Many terminals ignore the macOS High Contrast setting entirely, leaving borders and text unchanged. Some terminals require manual theme switching to achieve higher contrast.

Chau7 actively adapts borders, text weight, selection highlighting, cursor visibility, and focus rings when High Contrast is enabled. Chau7 also preserves your chosen theme character while boosting readability, so you do not need to switch to a separate high-contrast theme.

Why Chau7 high contrast mode matters

Low-vision developers and anyone working in challenging lighting conditions benefit from enhanced contrast. Chau7 respects the macOS High Contrast setting automatically, so accessibility is built in rather than bolted on.

Chau7 adjusts borders, text weight, and UI element contrast without requiring manual theme switching. This zero-configuration approach means accessibility works from the first launch.
