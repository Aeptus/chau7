How do you say Chau7?

show · set
/ʃo.sɛt/

It's French for "sock". 🧦
Yes, really. Your terminal is named after a sock.

The math

chau + 7 (sept) = chaussette = 🧦

Sept is the French word for 7. Stick it onto chau and you get chaussette, the common French word for sock. Computer science has never been this cozy.

A survival guide for non-French speakers

Syllable 1
show
Like a TV show.
Not "chow". Not "cow". Not "chaos".
Syllable 2
set
Like "set a variable".
Developers already know this one.
Put together
show-set
Rhymes with "no sweat".
Two syllables. You got this.

Memory tricks

  • 🎬

    The Hollywood method

    "I'm watching a show, and I need to set up my terminal." Show-set. Done.

  • 🎵

    The musical method

    Sing it to the rhythm of "sunset". Same cadence. Show-set. Sun-set. Feel the vibe.

  • 🧦

    The sock method

    Just remember it means "sock" in French. Every time you open your terminal, you're putting on a sock. A very fast, GPU-accelerated sock.

  • 💡

    The "no sweat" method

    "Show-set" rhymes with "no sweat". And using Chau7? Also no sweat. The mnemonic writes itself.

Why name a terminal after a sock?

Some apps are named after Greek gods. Others after celestial phenomena. We went with an insider joke from a French MP3 saga.

If you grew up in France in the 2000s, you know Le Donjon de Naheulbeuk, a hilariously chaotic fantasy audio saga that an entire generation downloaded on eMule and passed around on burned CDs. It's the French equivalent of discovering Monty Python, except everyone has a D20 and nobody agrees on initiative order.

Chaussette is a running gag in the saga: absurd, out of place, and unforgettable. If you know, you know. If you don't, you just downloaded a terminal named after a sock and that's also pretty funny.

Plus, chaussette is a seven-letter pun hiding a number. If you're going to name a developer tool, you might as well make it a puzzle. And this time it's not a bar… but that would be a great bar name.
